With hundreds of Sudoku apps on the Google Play Store, choosing the right one is not easy. Some are loaded with ads, some lack teaching tools, and some charge premium prices for basic features.

We compared three Sudoku apps that take notably different approaches to the classic puzzle. This is an honest comparison — we built Sudoku Spark, so we are transparent about that, but we also acknowledge where competitors genuinely do things well.

## What to Look for in a Sudoku App

Before diving into individual apps, here is what separates a great Sudoku app from an average one:

- **Hint quality** — Does it just reveal the answer, or does it teach you the technique behind it?
- **Price and monetization** — Is it truly free, or does it push subscriptions and in-app purchases?
- **Privacy** — How much personal data does the app collect?
- **Offline play** — Can you play without an internet connection?
- **Design** — Is the interface clean, modern, and easy to read?
- **Content** — Are there enough puzzles and difficulty levels to keep you challenged?

### Sudoku.com — Classic Sudoku

**Developer:** Easybrain | **Price:** Free with in-app purchases | **Downloads:** 50M+

Sudoku.com is the most downloaded Sudoku app on Android. Backed by Easybrain (a major casual games publisher), it offers six difficulty levels, seasonal events, and tournaments that keep the experience fresh, along with the largest player community in the category.

**Strengths:**

- Six difficulty levels including Fast and Giant modes
- Seasonal events with collectible trophies and medals
- Tournaments for competitive players
- Available on both Android and iOS
- Large, established community with 2M+ reviews
- Google Play Pass compatible

**Limitations:**

- Hints reveal the answer without explaining the solving technique
- Collects significantly more data: location, financial info, and other categories
- Frequent prompts to upgrade to premium or subscribe for an ad-free experience
- Interface can feel busy with promotional banners and event pop-ups

[View Sudoku.com on Google Play](https://play.google.com/store/apps/details?id=com.easybrain.sudoku.android)

### Sudoku Coach

**Developer:** Maitrejean | **Price:** $9.99 (Lite version free) | **Downloads:** Niche

Sudoku Coach is the most education-focused Sudoku app available. It teaches over 54 solving strategies through animated, step-by-step visual explanations. It is designed less as a game and more as a serious learning tool for people who want to deeply understand Sudoku logic.

**Strengths:**

- 54 animated solving strategies — the most comprehensive teaching library of any Sudoku app
- Step-by-step graphical explanations for advanced techniques including 3D Medusa, Forcing Chains, and Death Blossom
- Input your own puzzles from newspapers or books and get solutions with explanations
- Extremely lightweight (2 MB)
- Highly rated (4.9 stars)

**Limitations:**

- Full version costs $9.99 — the free Lite version only covers 10 basic strategies
- Utilitarian design that prioritizes function over visual appeal
- Teaching style uses advanced competition terminology (3D Medusa, Death Blossom, Exocet) that may feel intimidating for casual players and beginners
- No daily challenges, streaks, or gamification features
- Smaller puzzle library compared to mainstream apps
- Last updated April 2025

[View Sudoku Coach on Google Play](https://play.google.com/store/apps/details?id=be.ema.sc)
